• Infant age:
    Time since birth at which serum bilirubin levels were sent

 

  • Total bilirubin levels:
    Bilirubin levels refer to total bilirubin. Direct bilirubin is not subtracted from the total unless it is >50% of the total bilirubin.

 

  • Gestation age:
    It's is calculated as the age in weeks at the date of birth since the first day of LMP (last menstrual period)

    Also it can be calculated from EDD (expected delivery date) as it is 40 weeks

    Eg:- If EDD is 28th August
    And
    Date of birth is 7th August then the gestation age is 37 weeks 

If the date of birth is 9th August then the gestation age is 37 2/7, for calculating it is taken as 37 weeks as 37 weeks are complete and 38th is ongoing and not complete

 

  • Phototherapy:
    Phototherapy preferable with blue light lamps/bulbs (425-475nm wavelength)
    Or with fibreoptic source

    Measure irradiance regularly at the skin by radiometer
    Minimum 5microW per square centimeter
    Max 9 microWPer square cm

    If using blue lamp standard distance between lamp and the baby should be 45 cm

    Follow the local clinical practise guidelines for baby care when under phototherapy

    Change phototherapy bulbs as per manufacture recommendation or when irradiance is below minimum 

 

  • Continuous multiple phototherapy:
    Initiate continuous multiple phototherapy if 

            -serum bilirubin levels are rising rapidly >0.5mg/dl per hour(8.5 micromol/dl per hour) 

            -serum bilirubin levels are within 3mg/dl ( 50 micromol/dl) below the threshold for which exchange transfusion is indicated at 72 hours 

            -serum bilirubin levels continues to rise or does not fall within 6hours of starting single phototherapy 

 

  • Exchange transfusion:
    While preparing for exchange transfusion when bilirubin levels are above threshold or in the multiple phototherapy range consider IVIG (500mg/kg over 4 hours)for ABO Rh incompatibility with coombs positivity and rise of bilirubin more than 0.5mg/dl per hour (8.5micromol/l per hour) 

Note: Follow the local clinical practice guidelines for newborn care during exchange transfusion

 

  • Non physiologic hyperbilirubinemia:

            -Onset of jaundice before 24 hours of age 

            -Any elevation of serum bilirubin that requires phototherapy

            -A rise in serum bilirubin levels over (0.5 mg/dl (8.5 micromol/l) per hour)

           -Signs of underlying illness in any newborn
            Vomiting, lethargy ,temperature instability, apnea ,tachypnea , poor feeding and excessive weight loss.

           -Jaundice persisting after 8 days in a term newborn and 14 days in a preterm newborn


The above mentioned conditions imply non physiologic hyperbilirubinemia and warrant investigation
